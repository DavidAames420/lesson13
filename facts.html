The World's Health Organization estimates that 300 million people suffer from asthma and is the most chronic disease among children. Asthma un diagnosed and under treated can create a substantial burden to indivduals and families. Over 80% of asthma deaths accour in low and lower-middle income countries. Asthma is caused by indoor allegens such as dust mites in beddings, carpets, and pet dander, pollens, moulds, tabacco smoke and even medicines such as aspirin. In some extreme cases asthma can also be triggered by cold air intese emotional arousal such as anger or fear and physical exercise. Although there is no cure for asthma it can be kept under control the disease enabling the patient to live a fufilling comfortable life. In short-term medications can relieve symptoms such as Albuterol, and Advair. Medicine is not the only way to keep asthma under control. A patient can prevent asthma attacks understands what triggers he or she should avoid. Althought asthma is not the most deadly disease on the planet left unchecked can lead to death. 250,000 people die from asthma annual and is estimated that the number of people with asthma will grow by more that 100 million by 2025. Asthma cost the US about 3,300 per person with the disease each year from 2002 to 2007 in medical expenses, missed school and work days and early deaths. The cost of asthma in the us grew about $53 billion in 2002 to about 56 billion in 2007. In 2008 asthma hospitalizations were 1.5 times higher among female than male patients. Among the 830 influenza-related pediatric deaths between 2004 and 2012, 16% of the subjects had asthma. 44% of 1160 children hospitalized for pandemic H1N1 infection had asthma.

    Fun Facts!

  • Asthmatic attacks can be triggered by allergens,exercise, smoke, pollution, or airway infections.
  • As of 2012, more than 18 million adults and 6 million children suffered from asthma.
